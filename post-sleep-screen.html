How Screen Time Affects Your Sleep (and What to Do About It)

Late-night screen use can delay sleep by keeping your brain alert and reducing the natural release of melatonin. That can lead to lighter, shorter, and less restorative sleep.

Create a one-hour digital sunset before bed. Dim lights, keep your phone outside the bedroom, and switch to calm offline activities like reading or journaling.

Even a few nights of consistent screen limits can improve energy, mood, and morning focus.
